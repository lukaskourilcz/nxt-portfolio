# Generative media production

This document is the provider-research gate, production contract, opportunity
audit, prompt foundation, and provenance manifest for custom portfolio media.
No provider is preferred by default. Generated work supplements real evidence;
it never replaces the authentic portrait, product screenshots, or public
project facts.

## Opportunity audit

| Opportunity | Classification | Decision |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Banking modernization cover | High value | Produce a clearly abstract migration-and-boundary composition; never imply access to a real banking interface. |
| Ersilia workflow cover | High value | Produce a paper-to-schema-to-validation composition without scientific findings, molecules, or fabricated charts. |
| devShark presentation environment | High value | Produce surroundings only, then composite the existing screenshot deterministically so every UI pixel remains authentic. |
| Hero system-boundary layer | Potentially useful, not selected | The authentic portrait and editorial proof list already provide one clear focal point. Extra above-fold media would add weight and compete with identity. |
| Case-study social imagery | Potentially useful, not selected | Keep the code-rendered localized OG system until a generated background materially improves small-preview legibility. Generated lettering is prohibited. |
| Homepage social image | Unnecessary | The current code-rendered image is localized, crisp, fast, and consistent with the identity. |
| Personal mark and favicon | Unnecessary | The code-native LK mark is scalable and recognizably part of the design system. |
| Motion and video | Unnecessary | Static covers explain enough; motion would add delivery cost without improving comprehension. |
| Portrait, people, banking UI, scientific results, fake product UI | Harmful or inappropriate | Never generate. |

## Shared art direction

The concept is **engineered editorial evidence**: technical publishing,
architectural boundaries, system maps, controlled print texture, and precise
dark-field composition. Use strong negative space, orthographic or front-facing
geometry, crisp layers, matte blue-black surfaces, restrained off-white, muted
steel, and one green signal. Texture may resemble fine paper grain, registration
marks, or anodized material, but must remain quiet.

Avoid cinematic science-fiction perspective, glass, neon clouds, cyberpunk,
random particles, floating code, fake terminal text, dashboards, charts,
generated interface labels, logos, people, hands, robots, brains, glowing chips,
purple-blue gradients, city skylines, watermarks, and decorative lettering.

### Reusable positive foundation

> Engineered editorial evidence, precise abstract technical publishing,
> front-facing or shallow orthographic composition, strong negative space,
> modular system boundaries, controlled routing and validation marks, matte
> blue-black field, restrained steel and near-white structure, one small green
> signal accent, crisp geometry, fine print grain, quiet premium finish, no
> readable text.

### Reusable negative foundation

> No people, faces, hands, offices, laptops, fake UI, dashboards, banking
> screens, scientific charts or findings, molecules, code, letters, numerals,
> words, logos, watermarks, neon, purple gradients, holograms, glassmorphism,
> particles, cyberpunk, dramatic perspective, random symbols, or glow clouds.

## Production briefs

### Banking modernization

- **Routes:** homepage Selected Work and both localized banking case studies.
- **Purpose:** communicate controlled modernization through boundaries,
  contracts, mapping, tests, and gradual migration without resembling a product
  screenshot.
- **Master:** 16:9, text-free, with a central mapping boundary and left/right
  crop safety. Produce web desktop and mobile crops from the selected master.
- **Semantics:** decorative; the existing localized figure caption explains
  that the material is abstract.
- **Budget:** one responsive image family, lazy outside the first viewport,
  target under 160 KB desktop and 100 KB mobile.

### Ersilia scientific metadata workflow

- **Routes:** homepage Selected Work and both localized Ersilia case studies.
- **Purpose:** show documents becoming structured, reviewed metadata through an
  explicit validation path—not discovery, prediction, or scientific results.
- **Master:** 16:9, text-free, with paper-like source layers, a structured schema
  field, and a restrained validation trace. Keep mobile crop safety near center.
- **Semantics:** decorative; the localized caption identifies an abstract
  project visual.
- **Budget:** one responsive image family, lazy outside the first viewport,
  target under 160 KB desktop and 100 KB mobile.

### devShark authentic presentation environment

- **Routes:** homepage Selected Work and both localized devShark case studies.
- **Purpose:** frame the real devShark screenshot as an owned product artifact
  without redrawing or stylizing its interface.
- **Master:** 16:9, text-free surroundings with a single centered blank display
  aperture. Composite `public/projects/devshark.png` locally and
  deterministically after selection.
- **Semantics:** informative because the final composite contains authentic UI;
  preserve the localized content alt text.
- **Budget:** target under 190 KB desktop and 120 KB mobile.

## Production workflow

1. Re-read this document and the real case-study content.
2. Complete the provider research below before recommending or using a service.
3. Preflight the selected provider's complete expected cost and export contract.
4. Generate materially different directions, not cosmetic prompt variations.
5. Inspect every result for text, fake UI, malformed geometry, branding,
   watermarks, misleading implications, crop safety, and brand consistency.
6. Refine only the strongest direction. Never accept the first usable output by
   default.
7. Download only selected masters and documented rejected references.
8. Create responsive AVIF/WebP exports with stripped metadata and intrinsic
   dimensions. Composite authentic UI deterministically.
9. Integrate through `next/image`, preserve code-native fallbacks until the
   selected family passes visual and release QA, then remove dead fallback code.
10. Record provider, model, prompt, generation reference, selection reasoning,
    export paths, dimensions, sizes, and regeneration instructions below.

## Mandatory provider research

A future agent must browse current official sources before recommending a paid
plan or using a generator. The research must be dated and must compare at least
three viable services when three exist: at least two genuinely free or low-cost
options and the strongest suitable paid baseline.

Record this table before generation:

| Provider | Official sources | Free allowance and expected project cost | Export resolution and ratios | Watermark | Commercial rights and ownership | Public/private generation, retention, training, deletion | Access route | Decision |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Pending research | Pending | Pending | Pending | Pending | Pending | Pending | Pending | Pending |

Use official pricing, terms, license, and privacy pages rather than remembered
facts or affiliate comparisons. Estimate the cost of all three selected asset
families and their comparison/refinement variants—not just one sample image.
Prefer a free or cheaper provider only when it permits commercial portfolio use,
adequate export quality, acceptable privacy, controllable aspect ratios, and
watermark-free delivery.

Reject providers with unclear commercial rights, mandatory public uploads of
source material, mandatory watermarks, insufficient output resolution, or no
reasonable deletion/privacy controls. Never upload the portrait, real product
UI, confidential banking material, customer communication, or unpublished
content. Never start a card-backed trial, purchase credits, or accept recurring
billing without explicit owner approval. If no suitable service exists, retain
the code-native visuals.
